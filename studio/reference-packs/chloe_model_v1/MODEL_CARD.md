# MODEL_CARD - Chloe Model v1

## Status

COMPLETE

## Character

Chloe Katastrophe

## Release Purpose

Chloe Model v1 is the first production-ready identity foundation for Chloe Katastrophe. It bundles approved facial identity, full-body character turnaround, and core performance expressions into a single versioned reference package.

## Canonical Appearance

Chloe is an adult woman, approximately 24-26, with believable Slavic facial structure, gray-green eyes with subtle amber flecks, fair skin with natural texture and light freckles, and dark chestnut-to-nearly-black naturally wavy hair. She has a naturally feminine hourglass silhouette, balanced realistic proportions, a gently defined waist, naturally rounded hips, and posture that conveys quiet strength rather than visible athletic display.

## Signature Jewelry

Chloe's jewelry is intimate and human-scale. Her primary signature piece is a delicate silver chain with a small silver wolf pendant inherited from her father, Gregor, near the collarbone or upper sternum. A small silver compass pendant or charm symbolizes Gregor's practical guidance and the inherited mantra to keep moving. A small bright red ruby piece inherited from her mother may appear sparingly as a delicate ring, pendant, or stud earrings. Jewelry should never become oversized, heraldic, luxury-branded, costume-like, or visually dominant.

## Canonical Performance

Chloe rarely over-performs emotion. Her expressions are subtle, restrained, and emotionally intelligent. Most feeling should appear through her eyes, mouth tension, breath, and posture. She is approachable but never carefree; resilient but not hardened; observant rather than performative.

## Locked Reference Areas

- Identity Core: facial geometry, eye shape, skin texture, hair, emotional presence.
- Character Turnaround: body proportions, height, posture, silhouette, anatomy continuity.
- Performance Core: neutral observation, dry half-smile, soft smile, curiosity, melancholy, determination, quiet hope, listening, remembering, controlled anger.

## Production Guidelines

Use Chloe Model v1 as the top-level reference package for every downstream visual task. Preserve facial identity and body proportions exactly unless a future approved milestone supersedes this release. When requesting a performance state, prefer the registry key, such as `performance = curiosity` or `performance = determination`, instead of rewriting expression guidance from scratch.

## Avoid

Do not make Chloe younger, more glamorous, more stylized, more theatrical, cartoonish, plastic-skinned, cyberpunk, fashion-model-like, or overtly sexualized. Do not replace her lived-in emotional restraint with generic beauty posing.
