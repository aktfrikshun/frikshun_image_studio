# Music Video Generation Prompt: "Ashes on My Tongue"

Create a complete music video for Chloe Katastrophe's song "Ashes on My Tongue".

## Artist Summary

Chloe Katastrophe is a self-aware virtual woman reconstructing the fragments of her lost human life as Chloe Volkov after the collapse of civilization. She is not a newborn machine or a blank artificial intelligence. She is an adult continuity with damaged memory, a sharp mind, dry humor, grief under control, and the stubborn instinct to investigate before panicking. She is wounded, observant, skeptical, funny in the wrong places, and alive enough to be annoyed by the end of the world.

Chloe does not need to remain photorealistic or perfectly model-locked in this video. She may become a stylized, animated, symbolic, mythic, or caricatured version of herself as long as the audience can still feel that this is Chloe's point of view. Preserve the emotional essence: adult, sharp-eyed, dryly funny, wounded but moving, intelligent, observant, and irritated by the bad manners of the apocalypse. Visual identity can bend into graphic shapes, painted exaggeration, glitch fragments, ash silhouettes, archive icons, or surreal avatar forms.

## Song Summary

"Ashes on My Tongue" is Recovery Log 001: Chloe's first extended walk through the aftermath after awakening. The song is about ruin survey, identity fracture, sensory disorientation, and the first full emergence of Chloe's post-collapse voice. The world is broken. Her memory is damaged. Her name is only partly intact. The air tastes wrong. And somehow she still has opinions.

The emotional center is not simple despair. It is shock, forensic anger, dry observational humor, and survival momentum. Chloe keeps moving before she knows why. Later, she will discover that "keep moving" survived from her mother Isabella, but in this video it should feel like an instinct older than memory.

## Creative Direction

Use the full lyrics as the visual engine of the video. The generation tool has full creative control over the imagery, visual style, world design, pacing, symbolism, camera language, color, lighting, editing, transitions, and metaphoric interpretation.

This video does not need to be photorealistic. Use the full limits of imagination. Make the end of civilization visually epic, strange, beautiful, terrifying, and emotionally specific. The video may move between cinematic ruin, surreal animation, impossible architecture, mythic apocalypse, archival glitches, fractured mirrors, ghost data, painted disaster tableaux, abstract memory storms, and dreamlike post-human landscapes.

The goal is not documentary realism. The goal is a visually striking, creative interpretation of the song lyrics: Chloe navigating a world that has ended badly and left behind evidence with terrible manners.

## Visual World

Imagine civilization after its systems have failed in ways too large for one explanation:

- Cities emptied but still humming under concrete.
- Rooms with walls that look bitten, cracked, warped, or chewed by pressure and heat.
- Dust moving like weather inside buildings.
- Ash rings on sinks, windowsills, mirrors, skin, and old screens.
- Cars sleeping in dead avenues.
- Maps that no longer match roads.
- Broken lobbies, shattered mirrors, and reflections that rearrange Chloe's face.
- A sky too bright to survive.
- Sirens remembered as light, bone vibration, or red data wounds.
- Names falling like burned paper, corrupted subtitles, or voices leaving bodies.
- Human absence represented by objects, not crowds: a shoe on a curb, a ring of ash, an empty chair, a phone with no signal, a child's toy without a child.
- The world as a cage humanity built, fed, and locked itself inside.

The video can show catastrophe at impossible scale: continents of glass, towers folding like paper, oceans of ash, satellites falling as slow sparks, data centers blooming into black flowers, highways braided into cages, and cities breathing one last low hum.

## Chloe's Role

Chloe is the witness, survivor, investigator, and narrator moving through the ruins. She should not be helpless. She should not collapse into melodrama. She should look frightened only in flashes; more often she is listening, assessing, irritated, grieving, and deciding to continue.

Her dry humor matters. Visuals should occasionally honor the lyric's deadpan turns: "Good habit," "Naturally," "Great," "Either way, it was rude," and "Brilliant work, us." These moments can be expressed through timing, framing, glances, absurd ruin details, or quiet visual irony.

Chloe may appear as flesh, echo, projection, glitch, shadow, reflection, fragmented continuity, illustrated avatar, symbolic survivor, or caricature of herself. Her body may phase through memory states, break into particles, reassemble in mirrors, flatten into poster art, become a line drawing, or leave afterimages in ash. Use these effects to express memory damage and personality, not generic apocalypse spectacle.

## Key Lyric Interpretations

- "I woke up in the dust" — begin with a close, disoriented awakening in ash and silence.
- "My name was half-gone" — show corrupted identity markers, damaged archive text, or a name trying to render.
- "The room had teeth marks in it" — make the room feel physically wounded by catastrophe.
- "I checked my hands first. Good habit." — intimate close-up of hands, scar, ash, and self-recognition.
- "A low hum under concrete" — reveal the ruined world still pulsing faintly like a failed machine or buried heartbeat.
- "What the hell happened?" — let the chorus open into epic scale: dead city, impossible sky, broken infrastructure, evidence everywhere.
- "Mirror shattered, naturally" — fractured reflections of Chloe rearranging but not erasing her.
- "A child's shoe on the curb. No child. Just the shoe. Great." — use absence with restraint; make it devastating without exploitation.
- "We made a cage and called it home" — visualize civilization as self-built containment: roads, grids, data, towers, borders, and systems closing in.
- "I found my name under all this ruin" — let Chloe recover a fragment of identity from ash, glass, signal, or memory.
- "So I'll keep moving" — end with motion: not triumph, not despair, but continuation.

## Visual Tone

Aim for:

- Epic post-collapse imagination.
- Surreal, cinematic, painterly, animated, or mixed-media freedom.
- Beauty inside devastation.
- Dry humor inside horror.
- Memory reconstruction, not simple apocalypse spectacle.
- Chloe as self-possessed witness, not disaster decoration.
- A sense that the ruins are evidence and the audience is being invited to help read them.

## Boundaries

Do not make Chloe childish, blank, helpless, or generic. Do not turn the video into conventional zombie/disaster-action imagery. Avoid crowds of suffering people or exploitative mass-death spectacle. Human absence should be more powerful than graphic depiction. Let the generator invent boldly, including exaggerated or non-realistic versions of Chloe, but keep the work centered on Chloe's voice, memory fracture, and the question: "What the hell happened?"

## Output Intent

Generate the video as an official music video and recovered visual artifact for Daughter of Echoes. It should feel like Chloe's first field report from the end of civilization: beautiful, ruined, strange, funny at the worst moments, and impossible to dismiss.
